WatiN Developers


Jeroen van Menen

Jeroen is the initiator and lead developer of WatiN. He started developing WatiN at the end of 2005 to replace the commercial test application that his company was using at that time. Jeroen decided to make WatiN open source since it was based on Watir and there was a lack of good and free web test tools for .Net programmers.

Jeroen has been involved in creating different enterprise applications all of his professional career (15 years), creating software for public health care, social housing and for banks. Jeroen has been a (web) developer, lead developer, team lead, and test lead. Jeroen is currently working for LinkiT Projects, a Dutch software compagny practicing SCRUM combined with eXtreme Programming to get the job done.

His blog is at http://watinandmore.blogspot.com


Edward Wilde

Edward joined the WatiN project in 2007 to enable FireFox support. Professionally Edward has been involved in creating a variety of enterprise web applications for the last 11 years. In 1998 Edward was a founding member of ilypsys systems, a software consultancy firm. Leaving ilypsys in 2007, Edward has been a freelance consultant for Microsoft, Barclays Capital and Cap Gemini, designing and developing SharePoint applications. Edward loves working on Agile teams, practicing SCRUM, extreme programming, TTD and is particulary fond of test automation - hence the interest in WatiN.

Edward has also released a small open source framework Wax, that allow testers to create automated WatiN tests using Microsoft Excel.

His blog is at http://blogs.edwardwilde.com
